Reclaimed Timber Facility

TypeRenovation
LocationAlexandra, Sydney
FocusIndustrial Architecture, Reclaimed Material
StatusAcademic project

Overview

This project proposes a timber facility that reframes demolished and urban trees as valuable material resources. The design combines a showroom, co-working area, workshop, and material processing spaces to make the lifecycle of timber visible to visitors, designers, and clients.

Site
Process

Focusing on reclaimed timber from demolished buildings and felled urban trees, the facility is designed to adapt specialised processes for timber fabrication. The existing main structure and roof are retained to maintain stability and visual cohesion with the surrounding facilities. The façade, lightweight structures and dividing walls are redesigned and rearranged to support the new production line. The showroom functions not only as a display space for final products and material samples for clients, but also as a gallery that presents the second life of timber, transforming material from deconstructed buildings and cut-down urban trees into potential timber sources for fabrication.

Design Strategy

The showroom is organised around social connection and material education. Visitors can see, touch, and test real timber components, while workshops demonstrate how reclaimed timber can be cleaned, sorted, reused, and assembled into new architectural systems.
