Reclaimed Kitchen Module

TypePrototype
MaterialOffcuts + tiles
FocusNo waste
StatusBuilt study - Academic Project

Overview

A compact charcoal grill kitchen module that transforms offcuts and salvaged materials into a functional object. The design combines a timber benchtop, ceramic tile inlays, plywood storage, and aluminium frames.

Supervior: Amy Seo | Second Edition
Teammate: Quan Pham

2nd year Master of Architecture

Material Source

Material offcuts were collected during site visits to fabrication workshops over Sydney. Timber offcuts with different sizes anf types were picked up from Cranbrook Workshop while tiles were from Natural Brick and Gitani Stone. Other parts of mock-up like aluminium frames, oregon timber bars for benchtop were mainly collected from Second Edition.

Second Edition | Cranbrook Workshop | Natural Brick | Gitani Stone

Design process

The project demonstrates how small leftover materials can become design drivers rather than waste. Each material is selected for function, texture, and visibility.
