TypeRenovation

Overview

This project proposes a timber facility that reframes demolished and urban trees as valuable material resources. The design combines a showroom, co-working area, workshop, and material processing spaces to make the lifecycle of timber visible to visitors, designers, and clients.

Process


Reclaimed timber facility proposal located in Alexandra Canal Depot, City of Sydney, siting within a damaged industrial landscape, but it is still connected to water, birds, insects, plants, soils, air and Sydney's wider urban tree network.

Bird species connected to Alexandra Canal ecology. Source: Adapted from Water Stories, ArcGIS Story Maps.

Focusing on reclaimed timber from demolished buildings and felled urban trees, the facility is designed to adapt specialised processes for timber fabrication. The existing main structure and roof are retained to maintain stability and visual cohesion with the surrounding facilities. The façade, lightweight structures and dividing walls are redesigned and rearranged to support the new production line.

Specialised material flows are adjusted and designed to perfectly adapt the existing structures while maintain distinguished movements and functions for each material type.

Design

The showroom functions not only as a display space for final products and material samples for clients, but also as a public gallery that presents the second life of timber, transforming material from deconstructed buildings and cut-down urban trees into potential timber sources for fabrication.

Proposed showroom is designed with reusage of reclaimed timber on both structure, architecture, facade and interior elements. Bring another life for salvaged timber creates unique and dynamic details for the gallery and whole facility. Showroom structure frames are built by using sandwich-type combination of a new hardwood timber column in the middle with two reclaimed timber planks on sides. Demolished timber was categorised and marked based on types and size to fit different positions on structure frame.
